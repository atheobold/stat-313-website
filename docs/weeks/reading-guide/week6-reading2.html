Notes

  • Someone must first be chosen to be in a study and refuse to participate in order to have non-response bias.

  • There must be a valid reason for someone to lie or be untruthful to justify saying response bias is present. Yes, anyone could lie at any time to any question. Response bias is when those lies are predictable and systematic based on outside influences. An example would be surveying Cal Poly students and asking them if they have ever driven drunk.


True or False: Convenience sampling tends to result in non-response bias.


True or False: Volunteer sampling tends to result in response bias.
